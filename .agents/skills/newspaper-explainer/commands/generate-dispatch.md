Load the newspaper-explainer skill. Read ./templates/broadsheet.html and ./references/css-patterns.md to absorb the canonical structure, CSS class vocabulary, and palette. Then generate a full broadsheet newspaper edition for: $@

Gather the facts FIRST — read relevant files, run commands, collect every verifiable data point you will reference. Write journalistically: authoritative third-person prose, present tense for ongoing situations, past tense for completed events. Each article must carry a BYLINE with attribution and timestamp. The newspaper name, tagline, and masthead metadata should be derived from the subject matter and context (project name, domain, date, edition number).

Select which structural elements the subject warrants (masthead, ticker, lead story, sidebar, secondary stories, letters, dispatch board, colophon). The lead story receives the most important or most active item; secondary stories cover supporting developments; the dispatch board surfaces actionable items, pending decisions, and notices. NEVER EVER fabricate facts — every claim in the newspaper must be traceable to something you actually read or ran.

Write to ~/.agent/newspapers/ and open in the browser.
