Corrections Ledger

Every error, publicly logged. Every correction, timestamped. Every complaint, acknowledged.

LAST UPDATED: APRIL 10, 2026  |  VERSION 1.0

A publication that never publishes corrections is either perfect or dishonest, and it is not perfect. This page is the public, permanent record of every factual error this newsroom has acknowledged, along with the correction issued, the date, and the reason. It is also where you learn how to report an error in the first place.

How to report an error

Send an email to sahit.koganti@gmail.com with the subject line "Correction: [report title]". Include:

  • A specific reference to the statement you believe is wrong. URL plus a quoted sentence is ideal; screenshots are welcome.
  • What you believe the correct statement should be.
  • Any evidence — a link to an authoritative source, a document, an official record — supporting your correction.
  • Whether you would like to be credited as the reporter of the correction (by name or anonymously).

Corrections are reviewed in the order received. Simple factual errors (a date, a number, a name spelling) are typically fixed within 48 hours. Complex disputes involving interpretation or multiple sources may take up to 14 days. In every case you will receive a personal reply, whether or not the correction is accepted.

How corrections are handled

Confirmed factual errors

When an error is confirmed:

  1. The affected report is updated in place. The corrected passage is marked with a small note in the byline footer saying "Updated [date] to correct [brief description]".
  2. A new entry is added to the ledger below, including the original text, the corrected text, the reporter of the correction (if credited), and a link to the updated report.
  3. If the error affected a published dataset, the dataset is regenerated and the regenerated version is published alongside the prior version. Both versions are retained and distinguishable by a version stamp.
  4. If the error materially changed a headline, conclusion, or public claim, a note is published at the top of the home page for at least seven days.

Disputes that are not confirmed

Some complaints are not corrections — they are disagreements about interpretation, methodology, or editorial judgment. In those cases:

  1. The complaint is logged in this ledger with the label "Dispute" and a brief summary.
  2. The publisher issues a public response on the ledger explaining why the complaint does not result in a correction.
  3. The complainant is offered a right of reply — an opportunity to publish a full response alongside the original report.

Silent edits are forbidden

Typographical corrections — fixing a typo, adjusting punctuation, restoring a dropped word — may be made silently if they do not change meaning. Any change that alters, even slightly, the factual or interpretive content of a report must be logged publicly on this page. Silent content edits are treated as an editorial failure.

How to read the ledger

Each entry below has five fields:

  • Date: when the correction was issued
  • Report: link to the affected report
  • Type: Correction, Clarification, or Dispute
  • Summary: what was wrong and what it now says
  • Reporter: who flagged it (self-reported, named reader, or anonymous)
