Support The Record

This publication runs on no advertising, no subscription paywalls, and no party funding. It survives on reader donations, the occasional grant, and a commitment to staying small.

Why support an independent newsroom? The short answer is that independent reporting costs money — in scraping infrastructure, data licensing, hosting, legal fees when someone contests a finding, and simply the opportunity cost of the hours the work takes. The Amaravati Record does not carry display advertising because it would create conflicts of interest with the subjects of our reporting. It does not operate a paywall because we want our work to be cited, translated, and republished as widely as possible. That leaves a small number of paths for keeping the lights on. Here they are.

One-time donations

The simplest way to support this publication is a one-time donation in any amount. These go directly to the publisher and fund: API costs (Gemini classification is the largest running expense), domain and infrastructure, legal consultation when it becomes necessary, and occasional travel for on-the-ground reporting.

Institutional support — grants

The Amaravati Record is eligible to receive grant funding from institutions that support independent public-interest journalism. If you represent a foundation, fellowship programme, or media-innovation fund and would like to consider supporting this work, please reach out to the editor.

Programmes we are a plausible fit for — and which readers are welcome to nominate us to:

  • Google News Initiative (India track)
  • Pulitzer Center data journalism grants
  • Internews / ICFJ fellowships
  • DataLEADS, DigiPub News India Foundation
  • IJNet media-innovation programmes
  • Academic research grants on Indian governance, land rights, or data ethics

Dataset licensing and commissioned research

Our cleaned datasets — the APCRDA beneficiary roll, the surname directory, the caste classification outputs — are freely available under the Open Database License 1.0. Most researchers and journalists can use them directly at no cost.

For commercial users — a corporation that wants to integrate our data into a paid product, a news aggregator that wants exclusive access to pre-publication datasets, or an institution that requires a bespoke data-sharing agreement with warranty terms — a separate commercial licence is available. Commercial licence fees fund the newsroom's ongoing work. Please contact the editor with a clear description of the intended use.

We also take on commissioned research: if a university, NGO, or public-interest institution wants an investigation of a specific dataset or a specific question related to Amaravati, we can discuss a fixed-fee engagement. Commissioned work is published under the same editorial independence policy as all other work — the commissioning party does not get pre-publication review of findings, only the usual right of reply.

Syndication

News organizations that wish to republish a full investigation can do so freely under the CC BY 4.0 licence, which requires only attribution and a link back. You do not need permission. You do not need to pay.

For commissioned syndication — where you want an exclusive first-run or a customised version translated for your publication's style — please contact the editor.

Academic partnerships

If you are a university researcher studying Indian governance, land reform, caste and representation, or data journalism methods, The Amaravati Record welcomes partnerships. Possible formats:

  • Joint papers using our cleaned datasets as the empirical base
  • Student thesis projects with mentoring from the publisher
  • Co-authored methodology papers for data-journalism conferences
  • Guest lectures on the pipeline and its ethical considerations

Things we will not accept

For the avoidance of doubt, this publication does not accept:

  • Payment from any political party, political-action committee, or candidate
  • Payment from the Government of Andhra Pradesh, any of its departments, or APCRDA itself
  • Payment from any corporation whose operations are a subject of our reporting, including real-estate developers, construction firms, or any entity with a financial stake in the Amaravati capital-region land market
  • Payment that comes with any editorial condition, including pre-publication review, input on framing, or negative-coverage avoidance clauses
  • "Sponsored content" in any form

If any potential supporter would fall under the categories above and wishes to contribute anyway, please discuss the conflict with the editor in advance. In most cases the answer will be no, and the refusal will be logged in the corrections ledger as a matter of record.
