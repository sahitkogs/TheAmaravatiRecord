Cookie Notice

Every cookie this site may set, why it exists, and how to turn it off.

LAST UPDATED: APRIL 10, 2026  |  VERSION 1.0

Most pages on this site set zero cookies by default. The one exception is if you click "Accept" on our consent banner, in which case Google Analytics 4 is loaded and sets its own cookies to track returning visits. You can revoke that consent at any time. This page explains exactly what gets set, by whom, for how long, and why.

1. Strictly necessary cookies

We do not set any strictly-necessary cookies. This site is a static publication with no logins, no shopping cart, no session state, and no per-user personalization. Everything you see is identical for every visitor. There is therefore no technical need for cookies and none are set on page load.

One tiny exception: the consent banner itself stores your choice ("Accepted" or "Rejected") in localStorage, not a cookie, so we do not prompt you again on every page. localStorage is not transmitted to any server and is not technically a cookie, but for transparency we disclose it here.

2. Analytics cookies — only if you consent

If and only if you click "Accept" on the consent banner, we load Google Analytics 4 with IP anonymization enabled. GA4 then sets the following cookies:

_ga Pseudonymous client ID. Used to distinguish unique visitors across sessions. Expires after 2 years from last visit.
_ga_<CONTAINER-ID> Session state. Used to identify an active browsing session (so that a single visit that spans several pages is counted as one session, not many). Expires after 2 years from last visit.

No other cookies are set by this site or any script loaded from it. We do not use Google Ads, Google Tag Manager, Facebook Pixel, LinkedIn Insight, Hotjar, Mixpanel, Amplitude, or any other third-party tracking or marketing service.

3. What Google Analytics does with these cookies

When analytics consent is granted, Google Analytics 4 processes the following on every page view:

  • The URL and title of the page you are viewing
  • The URL of the page that referred you (if any)
  • Your IP address, anonymized to the /24 block before storage (so the last octet is dropped — we cannot see your exact IP)
  • Your user agent string (browser + OS)
  • Approximate geographic location derived from the anonymized IP (country, and sometimes region — never city-level from an anonymized IP)
  • The pseudonymous client ID from the cookies above
  • Timing information about how long the page took to load

We use this data in aggregate to answer questions like "how many people read the land-pooling investigation" and "which referring sites send us the most traffic". We do not attempt to identify individual readers, and we do not have any tool that would let us.

Google, as the analytics provider, may retain and process the data under their own terms. IP anonymization is enabled at the client side, before the data is sent, so Google never receives your full IP address from our site. Data is retained for 14 months, the minimum GA4 setting, then automatically purged.

4. How to reject or revoke consent

Reject at first visit

When you first land on the site, a banner appears at the bottom of the screen asking whether to enable analytics. Click "Reject". No analytics cookies will be set and no data will be sent to Google Analytics. The site works identically.

Revoke a previous acceptance

If you previously clicked "Accept" and want to change your mind, any of these work:

  • Click the "Cookie settings" link in the footer of any page — the banner will reappear and you can switch your choice.
  • Clear cookies for this site in your browser — the next page load will prompt you again.
  • Install a browser extension like uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger — both block GA4 at the network level regardless of our banner.

Block globally

If you want to reject analytics across all sites you ever visit, install the Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on provided by Google itself. This is the simplest way to ensure GA never sees you on any site, not just ours.

5. First-party vs third-party cookies

Google Analytics 4 cookies are set on the domain sahitkogs.github.io — they are technically first-party cookies for this site. They are not shared with other sites. However, the data they carry is processed by Google (a third party) under Google's terms. We disclose this as a third-party data processor in the Privacy Policy.

6. Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control

If your browser sends the DNT: 1 header or the Sec-GPC: 1 header, our consent banner treats that as an automatic "Reject" — no analytics are loaded, no consent prompt is shown. These signals are honored silently.
