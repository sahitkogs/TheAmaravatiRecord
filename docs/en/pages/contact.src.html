Contact & Tip Line

The different ways to reach this newsroom — from casual correspondence to encrypted whistleblower submissions.

The Amaravati Record is a one-desk independent newsroom. Every email comes to one address and is read by one person — which is both a feature (no gatekeepers, no form letters) and a limit (response times depend on publication cycles). Here is how to reach us for different purposes.

Routine correspondence

For questions about reports, requests to republish, academic citation queries, general feedback, and anything else that does not require confidentiality:

Editor
sahit.koganti@gmail.com

Reply time: usually within 48 hours; longer if a major report is in production.

Corrections

To report a factual error in a published report, use the same email address with the subject line Correction: [report title]. The full process, including how we log and acknowledge corrections, is on the Corrections Ledger page.

Right of reply

If you are named in a report and wish to publish a response, email with the subject line Right of reply: [report title]. Replies are handled according to the Editorial Policy, section 8.

Data and dataset requests

For researchers, academics, NGOs, or other newsrooms who want to reuse our cleaned datasets:

  • Datasets are published under the Open Database License 1.0. You do not need permission to reuse them — attribution and share-alike on derivatives are the only obligations.
  • If you need a dataset in a different format, a specific subset, or a version with privacy fields unmasked under a data-sharing agreement (for legitimate academic research with ethics approval), email with the subject line Data request: [purpose]. Academic requests are handled on a case-by-case basis and may require a signed non-disclosure agreement for unmasked data.

Confidential tips and whistleblower submissions

This newsroom takes source protection seriously and treats it as a professional duty that supersedes any editorial convenience. If you have a tip, a leaked document, an internal memo, or a first-hand account that bears on capital-region land, governance, construction, or public finance, and you need to protect your identity, here are the options in order of increasing anonymity.

1. Encrypted email (PGP)

Send an encrypted email using the editor's PGP public key. This protects the content of the message from anyone reading Gmail, but does not hide the fact that you sent mail to this address (your email provider and ours can see the envelope).

Public key: pgp-key.txt (download and import into your mail client or GPG)

Fingerprint: [placeholder — will be published here once the key is generated]

If you do not already use PGP, the friendliest starting point is Mailvelope for Gmail, or GPG Suite for macOS, or GnuPG on Linux/Windows. These take 10–20 minutes to set up.

2. Signal

If you prefer a phone-number-based encrypted channel, Signal is acceptable. [A Signal username will be published here if and when one is established. For now, request it via encrypted email and we will respond with a secure handle.]

3. Tor and anonymous intake (not yet operational)

For the highest level of anonymity — where even the fact that you contacted this publication must be hidden — a Tor-based anonymous intake (OnionShare or SecureDrop) is the industry standard. The Amaravati Record does not currently run a Tor hidden service because the operational-security discipline required is non-trivial for a one-person operation. If you have a tip that genuinely requires this level of protection, please send an encrypted email asking for instructions and we will coordinate a secure handoff.

Source protection commitments

If you are considered a source, this publication commits to:

  • Never revealing your identity to any private party, including our own lawyer, except under your explicit written instruction.
  • Revealing your identity to a court only upon direct court order after exhausting all appellate remedies.
  • Stripping metadata (authorship, revision history, timestamps, GPS tags) from any document you share before it is published or cited.
  • Not asking for personal information about you beyond what is strictly needed to verify the material.
  • Deleting our records of our correspondence with you once the story is published, unless you explicitly ask us to retain them.

Security researchers and responsible disclosure

If you have found a vulnerability in this site or its underlying repository, please report it through the channels documented in .well-known/security.txt. We commit to acknowledging reports within 72 hours and to not taking legal action against good-faith security research.

Legal correspondence

Defamation complaints, takedown requests, privacy-rights requests under DPDP Act 2023 or GDPR, and any other legal notices should be sent to the editor's email with the subject line Legal: [subject]. These are handled according to the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy.

Social media

This publication does not currently maintain social-media accounts. Any account claiming to represent The Amaravati Record on X, Facebook, Instagram, Threads, Bluesky, Mastodon, or similar platforms is unauthorized. The only official channels are this website and the editor's email address. If and when social-media accounts are established, they will be linked from the home page and this contact page, and verified here.

Physical address

This is a digital-first publication operated from India. There is no newsroom office. For formal legal service, the editor's email address is sufficient under Indian law; if paper service is required, contact us first to arrange an address.
