Privacy Policy

Plain-English summary of what data this publication collects, why, and your rights under Indian and international law.

LAST UPDATED: APRIL 10, 2026  |  VERSION 1.0

The Amaravati Record is an independent newsroom operated from India by Sahit Koganti. This policy explains what information this site collects from visitors, what we do with it, and the rights you have under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (India) and the General Data Protection Regulation (EU/UK). Where the two regimes differ, we follow whichever is stricter.

1. Who is responsible

The data fiduciary (under DPDP Act) and data controller (under GDPR) for this site is Sahit Koganti, an individual operating this publication in a personal capacity from India. There is no corporate entity behind it. For any privacy-related request, contact sahit.koganti@gmail.com.

2. What this site collects

Automatically, from everyone

  • HTTP request logs via GitHub Pages. This site is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub logs the IP addresses, user agents, and timestamps of visitors as part of normal web operation. These logs are retained by GitHub and are not accessible to us. See GitHub's Privacy Statement for their retention and use terms.
  • Font files from Google Fonts CDN. The site loads Playfair Display, Source Serif 4, and JetBrains Mono from fonts.gstatic.com. Google receives your IP address and user agent when these fonts are fetched. No cookies are set by the font loader itself. See Google's Privacy Policy.

Only if you consent (via the cookie banner)

  • Google Analytics 4. If (and only if) you click "Accept" on the cookie banner, we load Google Analytics 4, which sets cookies and sends your IP address, user agent, page path, referrer, and a pseudonymous client ID to Google. We use this data in aggregate to understand how readers find and use the site. IP anonymization is enabled. See the Cookie Notice for the exact cookie names and purposes.

If you do not consent, no analytics cookies are set and no data is sent to Google Analytics. The site works identically either way.

Only if you contact us

  • Email correspondence. If you write to sahit.koganti@gmail.com — whether to send a tip, ask a question, report a correction, or file a privacy request — your email address and the contents of the message are received by Gmail and retained according to Google's terms. We do not forward or share that correspondence.
  • Encrypted tips. If you use our PGP key to send an encrypted tip, the decrypted contents exist only in our local key-holder's machine. We do not log metadata about incoming encrypted mail beyond what Gmail does by default.

3. What this site does not collect

  • No account registration. There are no user accounts, logins, or passwords.
  • No tracking pixels, no advertising networks, no social-media "like" buttons, no embedded videos from third-party tracking platforms.
  • No third-party JavaScript beyond the specific, disclosed items above.
  • No server-side collection of personal data — this is a static site with no backend database under our control.

4. Why we collect what we do

  • Analytics: to understand which reports find readers, which sources refer traffic, and which countries our audience is in. This helps us decide what to cover next. The legal basis is your consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a); DPDP Act s. 7(a)).
  • Email correspondence: to respond to you. Legal basis is your consent or the legitimate interest of corresponding with you as you requested.
  • Server logs: retained by GitHub Pages for operational and security purposes (their legal basis, not ours).

5. Who we share data with

  • Google LLC — if you consent to analytics, Google Analytics 4 processes the data under their Data Processing Terms. IP is anonymized before storage.
  • GitHub, Inc. — operates the hosting infrastructure.
  • Nobody else. We do not sell, trade, or share data with any third party for marketing, advertising, or resale purposes. Ever.

If Indian or foreign law compels disclosure (subpoena, court order, lawful warrant), we will comply after consulting counsel. We will notify affected users where lawful to do so.

6. How long data is kept

  • Analytics data: 14 months in Google Analytics (the minimum GA4 retention); then automatically purged.
  • Email correspondence: retained indefinitely unless you ask us to delete it.
  • Encrypted tips: destroyed after the story is published, unless you tell us otherwise.

7. Your rights

Under the DPDP Act 2023 (India), you have the right to

  • Access the personal data we hold about you (s. 11)
  • Request correction or erasure (s. 12)
  • Nominate another individual to exercise your rights in the event of your death or incapacity (s. 14)
  • File a grievance and, if unresolved, approach the Data Protection Board of India (s. 13)

Under the GDPR (EU/UK), you additionally have the right to

  • Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, and data portability (Arts. 15–20)
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interest (Art. 21)
  • Withdraw consent at any time (Art. 7(3)) — you can do this by clicking "Reject cookies" in the banner, which revokes analytics consent immediately
  • Lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority

To exercise any of these rights, email sahit.koganti@gmail.com with the subject line "Privacy request". We respond within 30 days.

8. Children

This site is not directed at children under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. Under DPDP Act s. 9, consent for processing children's data requires verifiable parental consent, which we do not solicit. If you believe a child has sent us personal data, contact us and we will delete it.

9. International data transfers

Google Analytics and Google Fonts route data through Google's global infrastructure, which includes servers in the United States and the European Union. For transfers from the EU/UK, Google relies on its Standard Contractual Clauses. For transfers from India, no equivalent framework exists yet under DPDP Act; we disclose the transfer here and rely on your consent as the legal basis. If this posture changes under forthcoming DPDP rules, we will update this policy.

10. Changes to this policy

If we materially change this policy, we will update the "last updated" date at the top of the page and, for significant changes, post a notice on the home page for at least seven days. Minor typographical corrections do not count as material changes.
